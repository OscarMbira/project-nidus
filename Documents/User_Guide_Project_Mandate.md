# Project Mandate User Guide

## Overview

The Project Mandate is a pre-project document that captures the essential information needed to initiate a project. It's designed to be quick to create (1 hour to 1 day maximum) and contains only readily available information at the project conception stage.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Pre-project document (can exist before a project is created)
- Quick creation (1 hour to 1 day)
- Lightweight (short and sharp, not detailed)
- Triggers project creation when approved
- Precedes the Business Case

## Quick Start: Creating a Mandate (1 Hour)

### Step 1: Access Mandate Creation (5 minutes)

1. Navigate to **PMO Admin** → **Project Mandates** → **Create Mandate**
2. Or go directly to `/platform/mandates/create`

### Step 2: Fill in Basic Information (10 minutes)

**Required Fields:**
- **Mandate Title**: Short descriptive title (e.g., "New Customer Portal Development")
- **Purpose**: Why are we documenting this? (Minimum 20 characters)
- **Quality Priority**: Select from Balanced, Time, Cost, or Quality

### Step 3: Document Core Sections (30 minutes)

**Section 1: Purpose**
- Explain why this initiative is being documented
- Minimum 20 characters required

**Section 3: Background**
- Describe the context and need for the project
- Minimum 100 characters required
- Explain what problem or opportunity this addresses

**Section 4: Project Objectives**
- List measurable objectives
- Minimum 100 characters required
- Use SMART criteria (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)

**Section 9: Outline Business Case**
- High-level business justification
- Minimum 100 characters required
- Detailed financial analysis will come in the Business Case

### Step 4: Add Key Details (10 minutes)

**Deliverables:**
- Click "Add Deliverable" to add key deliverables
- Mark if in-scope or out-of-scope
- Indicate if major deliverable

**Stakeholders:**
- Click "Add Stakeholder" to identify customers/users
- At least one customer or user stakeholder is required for approval
- Mark primary stakeholders if applicable

**Proposed Roles:**
- Specify Proposed Executive (if known)
- Specify Proposed Project Manager (if known)
- At least one role is required for approval

### Step 5: Save and Review (5 minutes)

1. Click "Save" to save as draft
2. Review completion progress indicator
3. Complete any missing required sections
4. Check validation messages for errors or warnings

### Step 6: Submit for Review (Optional)

Once all required sections are complete:
1. Click "Submit for Review"
2. System validates all requirements
3. Mandate status changes to "submitted"

## Complete Guide: All 12 Sections

### Section 1: Purpose
**Purpose**: Why are we documenting this initiative?

**Guidelines:**
- Explain the reason for creating this mandate
- Describe the trigger that led to this documentation
- Minimum 20 characters

**Example:**
> "To document the initiative to develop a new customer portal that will improve customer self-service capabilities and reduce support costs by 30%."

### Section 2: Authority Responsible (Optional but Recommended)
**Purpose**: Who authorizes the costs and resources?

**Guidelines:**
- Identify the person or role responsible for authorization
- May not be known initially - can be added during review
- Recommended for approval

**Example:**
> "Chief Technology Officer (CTO) - Technology Department"

### Section 3: Background
**Purpose**: What is the context and need for this project?

**Guidelines:**
- Describe the current situation
- Explain what problem or opportunity exists
- Provide context for why this project is needed
- Minimum 100 characters required

**Example:**
> "Our current customer portal was built 5 years ago and lacks modern features. Customers frequently contact support for tasks they should be able to do themselves. A new portal with self-service capabilities will improve customer satisfaction and reduce operational costs."

### Section 4: Project Objectives
**Purpose**: What are we trying to achieve?

**Guidelines:**
- List measurable objectives
- Use SMART criteria:
  - **Specific**: Clear and unambiguous
  - **Measurable**: Can be quantified
  - **Achievable**: Realistic given resources
  - **Relevant**: Aligned with business goals
  - **Time-bound**: Has a deadline
- Minimum 100 characters required

**Example:**
> "1. Launch new customer portal by Q3 2026
> 2. Achieve 80% customer self-service rate within 6 months
> 3. Reduce support ticket volume by 30%
> 4. Improve customer satisfaction score from 7.5 to 9.0"

### Section 5: Scope
**Purpose**: What is included and excluded?

**Guidelines:**
- List major deliverables using the "Deliverables" section
- Document what is explicitly out of scope
- Be clear about boundaries

**Deliverables:**
- Add each deliverable with name and description
- Mark if in-scope or out-of-scope
- Indicate if major deliverable
- At least one in-scope deliverable required for approval

### Section 6: Constraints (Optional but Recommended)
**Purpose**: What limitations exist?

**Guidelines:**
- Resource constraints (budget, people, equipment)
- Time constraints (deadlines, milestones)
- Location constraints
- Technical constraints

**Example:**
> "Budget not yet approved. Project must use existing infrastructure. Must comply with GDPR regulations. Launch required before end of Q3 2026."

### Section 7: Interfaces (Conditional)
**Purpose**: What connections exist with other projects/programmes?

**Guidelines:**
- **Required** if project is part of a programme
- Document dependencies on other projects
- Document integrations with existing systems
- Can be optional for standalone projects

**Example:**
> "This project interfaces with:
> - CRM System Integration Project (data sync)
> - Authentication Platform (single sign-on)
> - Marketing Portal Project (shared design system)"

### Section 8: Quality Expectations
**Purpose**: What quality standards apply?

**Guidelines:**
- Select quality priority: Time, Cost, Quality, or Balanced
- Describe quality standards and expectations
- Recommended for approval

**Quality Priorities:**
- **Time**: Fast delivery is critical
- **Cost**: Budget constraints are primary concern
- **Quality**: High quality standards must be met
- **Balanced**: Equal importance to time, cost, and quality

### Section 9: Outline Business Case
**Purpose**: High-level business justification

**Guidelines:**
- Provide business reasons for doing this project
- Explain expected benefits
- High-level only - detailed financial analysis comes in Business Case
- Minimum 100 characters required

**Example:**
> "The new customer portal will improve customer satisfaction through better self-service capabilities, reduce operational costs by decreasing support ticket volume, and provide opportunities for upselling through targeted recommendations."

### Section 10: Associated Documents (Optional)
**Purpose**: Links to related documents

**Guidelines:**
- Link to estimates, feasibility studies, risk assessments
- Reference existing business cases if relevant
- Can be added during review process

### Section 11: Proposed Roles
**Purpose**: Who will lead this project?

**Guidelines:**
- Specify Proposed Executive (sponsor)
- Specify Proposed Project Manager
- **At least one role is required for approval**
- Can be names if users not in system yet

### Section 12: Customers and Users
**Purpose**: Who is impacted by this project?

**Guidelines:**
- Add stakeholders with type: Customer, User, or Interested Party
- **At least one customer or user is required for approval**
- Mark primary stakeholders
- Include contact information if available

## Workflow: Mandate → Project Creation

### Step 1: Create Mandate (Draft)
- Status: `draft`
- Can be edited freely
- Minimum fields required to save

### Step 2: Submit for Review
- Status: `submitted`
- All required sections must be complete
- Cannot be edited while under review
- Reviewers can provide feedback

### Step 3: Approval
- Status: `approved`
- Executive/PMO approves the mandate
- Mandate is ready for project creation
- Cannot be edited (locked)

### Step 4: Create Project from Mandate
- Click "Create Project" on approved mandate
- Review and confirm project details
- System creates project and links mandate
- Mandate becomes historical reference

### Step 5: Business Case Creation
- After project creation, create Business Case
- Business Case can reference the mandate
- Data from mandate can be copied to Business Case

## Validation Rules

### Draft (Save)
- Mandate Title: Required
- Purpose: Required (min 20 chars)

### Submission (Review)
- All draft requirements
- Background: Required (min 100 chars)
- Project Objectives: Required (min 100 chars)
- Outline Business Case: Required (min 100 chars)
- At least 1 deliverable
- At least 1 customer/user stakeholder

### Approval
- All submission requirements
- At least one of: Proposed Executive OR Proposed PM
- Quality priority selected (recommended)
- Interfaces (if part of programme - required)

## Best Practices

### Writing Effective Mandates

1. **Be Concise**: Keep sections short and focused
2. **Be Specific**: Use clear, measurable objectives
3. **Be Honest**: Don't hide constraints or challenges
4. **Think Forward**: Consider what information will be needed for Business Case
5. **Get Input**: Consult stakeholders while drafting

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Too Vague**: "Improve customer satisfaction" → Better: "Increase customer satisfaction score from 7.5 to 9.0 within 6 months"
2. **Missing Deliverables**: Always list at least key deliverables
3. **No Stakeholders**: Identify at least primary customers/users
4. **Unrealistic Objectives**: Ensure objectives are achievable
5. **Ignoring Constraints**: Document known limitations early

### Time Management

- **1 Hour Version**: Focus on Sections 1, 3, 4, 9, and key deliverables/stakeholders
- **Full Version**: Complete all 12 sections with detail
- **Progressive**: Can save as draft and complete over time

## Practice Mode (Simulator)

In the Simulator, you can practice creating mandates without affecting real projects:

- All mandates are clearly marked as "Practice Mode"
- Practice scores provide learning feedback
- Practice projects can be created from approved practice mandates
- Perfect for learning the process before working on real mandates

## Troubleshooting

### Can't Edit Mandate
- Check mandate status - only `draft` and `rejected` mandates can be edited
- If linked to project, mandate cannot be edited
- Check user permissions

### Can't Submit for Review
- Review validation errors
- Complete all required sections (marked with *)
- Add at least one deliverable
- Add at least one customer/user stakeholder

### Can't Approve Mandate
- Ensure all reviewers have completed reviews
- Verify all required sections are complete
- Ensure at least one role (Executive or PM) is specified
- Check quality priority is selected (recommended)

### Can't Create Project
- Mandate must be `approved`
- Mandate must not already be linked to a project
- Ensure at least one role (Executive or PM) is specified
