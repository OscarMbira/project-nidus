# Exception Report Templates and Examples

## Template Structure

Exception Reports follow a standard 8-section structure aligned with structured project management methodology.

## Section Guidelines

### Section 1: Document Information
- **Report Title**: Clear, descriptive title
- **Version**: Start with 1.0, increment by 0.1 for revisions
- **Urgency**: Match urgency to exception severity
- **Author/Owner/Client**: Assign appropriate roles

### Section 2: Exception Overview
- **Exception Title**: Brief, clear title
- **Summary**: 2-3 sentence summary
- **Tolerance Details**: Be specific with numbers and percentages
- **Variance**: Calculate accurately

### Section 3: Current Plan Status
- **Time Performance**: Current schedule status
- **Cost Performance**: Current budget status
- **Baseline vs Forecast**: Show clear comparison
- **Variances**: Calculate days and cost differences

### Section 4: Cause Analysis
- **Cause Description**: What happened (minimum 100 characters)
- **Root Cause**: Why it happened (minimum 100 characters)
- **Category**: Select appropriate category
- **Contributing Factors**: List all relevant factors

### Section 5: Consequences
- **Project Level**: Immediate project impacts
- **Programme Level**: Programme-wide implications
- **Corporate Level**: Organizational impacts
- **Business Case**: Quantify business case impact
- **Project Plan**: Detail plan impacts

### Section 6: Options Analysis
- **Minimum 2 Options**: Always provide alternatives
- **Each Option Should Include**:
  - Clear description
  - Business case impact
  - Risk assessment
  - Pros and cons
  - Feasibility rating
- **Recommendation**: Mark exactly one option

### Section 7: Recommendation
- **Summary**: Clear recommendation statement
- **Justification**: Why this option (minimum 100 characters)
- **Requested Decision**: Specific decision needed from board

### Section 8: Lessons Learned
- **Summary**: Overall lessons
- **Preventive Measures**: How to prevent recurrence
- **Individual Lessons**: Specific lessons with categories

## Example: Budget Overrun Exception

### Section 2: Exception Overview
**Exception Title**: Budget Overrun - Development Phase

**Summary**: The project has exceeded its development phase budget by 15% due to additional resource requirements and scope changes. The variance amounts to $45,000 against a baseline budget of $300,000.

**Tolerance Type**: Cost
**Tolerance Threshold**: $300,000 (baseline budget)
**Actual Value**: $345,000 (current forecast)
**Variance**: $45,000 (15%)

### Section 4: Cause Analysis
**Cause Description**: The budget overrun was caused by three primary factors: (1) Additional development resources were required to address technical challenges not identified during planning, (2) Scope changes approved during the phase added 20% more work, (3) Vendor cost increases for critical components exceeded initial estimates.

**Root Cause Category**: Planning
**Root Cause Analysis**: The root cause was insufficient risk assessment during the planning phase. Technical risks were underestimated, and the impact of approved scope changes on budget was not fully calculated before approval.

### Section 6: Options Analysis

**Option 1: Reduce Scope**
- Description: Remove non-critical features to bring budget back in line
- Business Case Impact: Some benefits will be deferred
- Risk Level: Medium
- Pros: Quick solution, maintains timeline
- Cons: Reduced functionality, stakeholder disappointment
- Feasibility: High

**Option 2: Request Additional Budget** (RECOMMENDED)
- Description: Request $50,000 additional budget to complete full scope
- Business Case Impact: All benefits realized, business case remains valid
- Risk Level: Low
- Pros: Full scope delivered, benefits realized
- Cons: Requires budget approval
- Feasibility: High

**Option 3: Extend Timeline**
- Description: Extend phase by 2 weeks to reduce resource costs
- Business Case Impact: Benefits delayed by 2 weeks
- Risk Level: Medium
- Pros: No additional budget required
- Cons: Timeline impact, resource availability
- Feasibility: Medium

### Section 7: Recommendation
**Recommended Option**: Option 2 - Request Additional Budget

**Justification**: Option 2 is recommended because it allows the project to deliver the full scope and realize all planned benefits. The additional $50,000 represents only 15% of the phase budget and is justified by the business case. The alternative options would compromise project value or create timeline risks.

**Requested Decision**: Approval to increase development phase budget by $50,000 to complete full project scope.

## Quality Checklist

Before submitting, ensure:

- [ ] All 5 quality criteria pass
- [ ] At least 2 options provided
- [ ] One option marked as recommended
- [ ] All required fields completed (minimum character counts met)
- [ ] Variances calculated correctly
- [ ] Business case impact assessed
- [ ] Root cause analysis completed
- [ ] Report submitted within 5 days

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Insufficient Options**: Always provide at least 2 well-analyzed options
2. **Missing Justification**: Recommendation must be justified (minimum 100 characters)
3. **Incomplete Analysis**: Root cause analysis must be detailed (minimum 100 characters)
4. **Late Submission**: Submit within 5 days of exception
5. **Incorrect Urgency**: Match urgency to exception severity
6. **Missing Impact Assessment**: Always assess business case and project plan impacts

## Best Practices

1. **Be Specific**: Use numbers, dates, and concrete details
2. **Be Objective**: Present facts clearly
3. **Be Complete**: Fill all relevant sections thoroughly
4. **Be Timely**: Submit promptly
5. **Be Clear**: Write for board members who may not be technical experts
