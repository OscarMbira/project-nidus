# Org Knowledge: Why EEF and OPA Belong in One Place

**Audience:** Project and programme leaders  
**Reading time:** ~3 minutes

Project success depends on what you **control**—and on what you **cannot**. Enterprise Environment Factors (EEF) capture those forces: regulation, culture, market shifts, and infrastructure. Organisational Process Assets (OPA) capture how your organisation works: templates, policies, procedures, and lessons learned.

Bringing both into **Org Knowledge** in Project Nidus gives teams a single place to **reference** and **improve** organisational context without mixing it into task lists or RAID logs.

## What you get

- **Shared visibility** across roles, with permissions tuned for viewers through admins.
- **Drafts and on-hold** for long-running capture without losing work.
- **Bulk import** from CSV for migration from spreadsheets or legacy tools.
- **Exports** for audits, board packs, and handovers—aligned with the rest of the app’s export patterns.

## Platform and Simulator parity

The same concepts apply to **live projects** and **simulation practice**. Records are stored in the appropriate schema (`public` vs `sim`), so learning data never pollutes production data.

## Next steps

Run the latest SQL migrations, open **Org Knowledge** from the sidebar, and start with a few high-impact EEFs (e.g. regulatory change) and OPAs (e.g. standard project template). Your future retrospectives will thank you.
