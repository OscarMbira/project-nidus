# PM Simulator User Guide

## Welcome to PM Simulator

The PM Simulator is an interactive platform designed to help you develop and practice project management skills through realistic simulations.

## Getting Started

### Creating an Account

1. Navigate to the PM Simulator
2. Click "Sign Up" or "Get Started"
3. Enter your email and create a password
4. Verify your email address (check your inbox)

### Your First Simulation

1. **Take the Skill Assessment**
   - Complete the initial assessment to determine your starting level
   - This helps personalize your learning experience

2. **Choose a Scenario**
   - Browse available scenarios by difficulty, industry, or methodology
   - Start with beginner scenarios if you're new to project management

3. **Select Your Role**
   - Choose a role that matches your learning goals
   - Different roles offer different perspectives and challenges

4. **Run the Simulation**
   - Make decisions based on events that occur
   - Receive real-time feedback on your choices
   - Complete all phases to finish the simulation

## Understanding Simulations

### Simulation Phases

Each simulation is divided into phases that represent different stages of a project:

- **Initiation**: Project kickoff and planning
- **Planning**: Detailed planning and resource allocation
- **Execution**: Active project work
- **Monitoring & Control**: Tracking progress and managing changes
- **Closure**: Project completion and lessons learned

### Events and Decisions

During a simulation, you'll encounter various events:

- **Stakeholder Requests**: Changes or new requirements
- **Resource Issues**: Team availability, budget constraints
- **Technical Challenges**: Technical problems or blockers
- **Risk Events**: Risks that materialize
- **Opportunities**: Chances to improve the project

For each event, you'll choose from multiple response options. Your choices affect:
- Project timeline
- Budget
- Team morale
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Overall project success

### Scoring System

Your performance is evaluated across multiple dimensions:

- **Timeline Management**: How well you manage project schedule
- **Budget Control**: Financial management effectiveness
- **Quality**: Deliverable quality and standards
- **Stakeholder Satisfaction**: Meeting stakeholder expectations
- **Team Management**: Leading and supporting your team

## Tips for Success

1. **Start with Beginner Scenarios**: Build foundational skills before tackling advanced challenges

2. **Read Event Descriptions Carefully**: Context matters when making decisions

3. **Consider Multiple Perspectives**: Think about how decisions affect different stakeholders

4. **Use Hints Wisely**: Hints are available but using them may affect your score

5. **Review Feedback**: After each decision, read the feedback to understand why choices were better or worse

6. **Practice Regularly**: Consistent practice improves your skills faster

7. **Join the Community**: Engage with other learners in forums and discussions

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I pause a simulation?**
A: Yes, your progress is saved automatically. You can resume from where you left off.

**Q: How long do simulations take?**
A: Beginner scenarios: 30-60 minutes. Intermediate: 1-2 hours. Expert: 2-4+ hours.

**Q: Can I retake scenarios?**
A: Yes, you can retake any scenario to improve your score and understanding.

**Q: Are certificates recognized?**
A: Certificates demonstrate your skills and can be shared on LinkedIn. They're not accredited certifications but show practical experience.

**Q: Can I use this for team training?**
A: Yes! Enterprise plans include team management and analytics for corporate training programs.
